Full Course Breakdown

6 Modules. One Complete System.

Each module builds on the last. Skip nothing. By the end, you produce work that brands pay for.
01
Audience, Script & Story Foundation
2 Parts · 67 Minutes
▼
Part 1 (18 min): How to identify a specific audience and their silent struggles — the foundation of every brand film, product video, and viral campaign. You'll learn to pick angles that make viewers stop scrolling and pay attention. Hook templates included.
Part 2 (49 min): The complete AI script-writing workflow. How to prompt AI to execute YOUR thinking — not think for you. How to refine, edit, and structure output into Hook → Problem → Story → Solution. Full walkthrough with a real example script.
02
Turning Script Into Human Voice
2 Parts · 36 Minutes
▼
Part 1 (23 min): The psychology of good voiceover — why pacing, silence, and emotion matter more than the tool. Why most AI voices sound robotic, and the exact settings and preparation that fix it. You'll understand voice before you generate a single word.
Part 2 (13 min): Hands-on inside the tool. Paste your script, choose voice, adjust speed and expressiveness. How to compare versions, pick the best one, and export correctly. By the end, your first voiceover is ready — and it sounds real.
03
Visual Storytelling & Image Creation
4 Parts · 88+ Minutes
▼
Part 1 (17 min): The psychology behind visuals that hold attention. Why images must match the emotion of the voice. Lighting, clarity, and why background must never distract. Understand this before you generate a single image.
Part 2 (13 min): Designing visual scenes from your script. The exact prompt structure: Subject, Emotion, Environment, Lighting, Style. Character consistency. Choosing between 3D, 2D, stickman, paper style — and when to use each.
Part 3 (58 min): The big one. Full live walkthrough — generate, adjust, regenerate, compare. How to fix wrong faces, modify lighting, avoid the most common beginner mistakes. Every image must pass one test: does it match the emotion of its voice line?
Part 4: Complete workflow applied to your own project. Not sample files — your actual script, your actual scenes. You leave this module with every visual ready.
04
Bringing Your Images To Life
2 Parts · 40 Minutes
▼
Part 1 (3 min): Short but essential. Motion psychology — why something moves, what each type of movement communicates, and why random movement destroys professional quality. Camera motion vs element motion. Watch this before you animate anything.
Part 2 (37 min): Full practical walkthrough. Animate your real Module 3 images — not test files. Keyframes, scaling, panning, parallax. How to keep motion subtle so it looks cinematic. How to match animation to the emotion of each scene. By the end, every scene is alive.
05
Assembling The Complete Story
3 Parts · Full Editing Workflow
▼
Part 1 (4 min): The mindset shift. Editing is not arranging clips — it is controlling attention. This short lesson changes how you think about every second of video. Watch it fully before you open any software.
Part 2 (15 min): Building the full video in CapCut — step by step. Arrange clips in the right order. Sync voice, images, and motion together. Pacing, timing, and making every second count.
Part 3: Putting it all together. The complete editing workflow applied to your project from start to finish. Your video is fully assembled by the end of this module.
06
Final Polish & Cinematic Refinement
3 Parts · 61+ Minutes
▼
Part 1 (8 min): The 8 psychology rules of final editing. Why videos become boring (equal timing kills engagement). What transitions actually mean. How music tells your viewer what to feel. Why silence hits harder than loud music. Why less effects always means more quality.
Part 2 (53 min): The longest lesson. Cinematic polish — music selection, volume balance, emotional timing. Sound effects that make animation feel physical. Transitions that serve the story. Pacing control so viewers never lose interest. Take your time with this one.
Part 3: The finishing line. Final subtitle styling, colour adjustments, export settings for maximum quality. The last check before your video goes live. After this — you have a client-ready video in your hands.
